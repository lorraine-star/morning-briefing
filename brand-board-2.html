The Vibe

Think the editorial pages of a high-end magazine — the kind you'd find on the desk of a Fortune 500 VP. Rich navy grounds the brand with authority, warm cream softens it, and the terracotta accent gives it life without shouting. This direction positions Lorraine as the definitive voice in her space — someone whose words carry weight because of how they're presented, not just what they say.

vs. Current Brand: A significant departure in feel. Moves away from tech-blue energy toward something more timeless and editorial. The Cormorant Garamond headline font is a statement — it says "author and thought leader" immediately. Higher perceived value, especially for enterprise and premium course pricing. The tradeoff: requires updating more assets since it's a bigger visual shift.
