The Vibe

This direction wraps professional expertise in warmth. The deep teal replaces blue with something that feels grounded and calming — like a trusted advisor you'd meet in a beautifully designed office, not a corporate conference room. Cream and sand tones create an approachable base, and the soft gold adds just enough polish. It reads as "I'm here to support your growth" rather than "I'm here to impress you."

vs. Current Brand: A full color departure — no blues at all. The teal-and-cream palette feels distinctly warmer and more personal, which could resonate strongly for course marketing and Instagram growth. Plus Jakarta Sans is modern and friendly without being childish. The risk: it's a bigger rebrand effort, and the teal-gold combination needs to feel distinctly "Lorraine" rather than generic wellness brand. The serif headlines help anchor it in authority.
